# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Hub! This directory contains the complete set of process documentation that guides how OctoAcme manages projects, coordinates teams, and delivers value to customers.

## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme follows an iterative, customer-focused approach to project management grounded in clear ownership, data-driven decisions, and cross-functional collaboration. Our project lifecycle consists of five key phases: **Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective**. The approach emphasizes psychological safety, transparent communication, and continuous learning. We maintain defined **roles** such as Project Managers, Product Managers, Developers, and QA specialists, each with clear responsibilities and communication channels. Weekly syncs between product and project leads, twice-weekly standups for delivery teams, and monthly stakeholder updates ensure alignment and early escalation of risks.

Our planning process transforms approved initiatives into actionable backlogs with clear acceptance criteria and estimated effort. Teams work in sprints or iterations, with each backlog item tracking ownership, dependencies, and integration points. We maintain a Risk Register to identify and mitigate technical and delivery obstacles early. The Definition of Done is documented upfront to ensure quality consistency, and release planning connects delivery increments to business milestones and customer value.

Execution focuses on iterative delivery of working software through daily standups, code reviews, and continuous feedback loops. Quality gates, test coverage, and observability are built into the process, not added afterward. Communication remains continuous and transparent—pull request descriptions, design docs, and code review comments provide the narrative of progress, decisions, and learnings.

After every release and at project closure, we conduct retrospectives to capture learnings, celebrate wins, and identify process improvements. This commitment to reflection and iteration ensures that OctoAcme projects become smoother and more predictable over time, while maintaining the flexibility to adapt to changing circumstances.

## Getting Started

**For new team members:** Start with the [Project Management Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md) to understand the big picture, then read [Roles and Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) to find your place in the process.

**For project leads:** Review [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md), [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md), and [Risks and Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md) to establish a solid foundation for your project.

**For delivery teams:** Lean on [Execution and Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) during sprint work, and refer to [Release and Deployment](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) as you approach launch.

**For continuous improvement:** Use the [Retrospective and Continuous Improvement](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md) guide at project closure to extract learnings and propose updates to these docs.

3. Propose a change or submit a pull request with your update.

All updates go through code review to ensure consistency and alignment with the broader project management approach.

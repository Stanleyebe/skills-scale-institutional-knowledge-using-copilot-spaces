# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Specialists

### Role Summary
QA and Testing Specialists ensure software quality by validating requirements, designing tests, and verifying acceptance criteria are met before release. They work closely with developers and product teams to maintain high quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Identify, document, and prioritize defects with reproducible steps
- Collaborate with developers on test automation strategies and coverage
- Validate fixes and conduct regression testing
- Report quality metrics and recommend process improvements
- Participate in release readiness reviews

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, reliable software to customers
- Reduce production incidents and post-release defects
- Improve test efficiency and test coverage over time
- Enable continuous delivery with confidence

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews with developers and product leads
- Defect reports and quality dashboards
- Test case documentation and automation reviews
- Release readiness assessments and sign-offs

---

## Technical Leads / Architects

### Role Summary
Technical Leads and Architects provide technical direction and design guidance. They help teams make sound architectural decisions, ensure scalability and maintainability, and identify technical risks early.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical vision and architectural approach
- Review and guide technical design decisions
- Mentor developers on best practices and code quality
- Identify and mitigate technical risks
- Ensure consistency across services and systems
- Support capacity planning and infrastructure decisions

### Goals
- Build scalable, maintainable, and secure systems
- Enable team growth through mentorship and knowledge sharing
- Minimize technical debt and rework
- Support long-term product sustainability

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Code review feedback and guidance
- Technical documentation and decision logs
- Escalation and risk mitigation discussions

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders represent business interests, customer needs, or strategic priorities. They provide input on requirements, approval on major decisions, and context on business impact.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements and success criteria
- Approve project scope, timeline, and budget decisions
- Escalate blockers and surface business constraints
- Communicate project status to leadership
- Validate solution fit for business needs
- Participate in major milestone reviews

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value and ROI
- Maintain alignment between product and business strategy
- Enable informed decision-making with business context
- Reduce scope creep and misalignment

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and briefings
- Scope and requirement reviews
- Decision and approval forums
- Strategic alignment meetings

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate deployment planning, release procedures, and go-live activities. They ensure smooth, predictable releases with minimal disruption.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases aligned with business needs
- Coordinate deployment with operations and support teams
- Maintain release notes and rollback procedures
- Verify deployment procedures and verify system health
- Communicate release status and any issues
- Document lessons learned post-release

### Goals
- Execute on-time, successful releases
- Minimize production incidents and customer impact
- Achieve smooth transitions and stakeholder confidence
- Build predictable, repeatable release processes

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and coordination meetings
- Deployment procedures and checklists
- Release notes and status communications
- Post-release retrospectives and metrics
